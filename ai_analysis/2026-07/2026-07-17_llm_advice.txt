### **4. Stocks to Keep an Eye On**

1.  **Oracle (ORCL):** While currently facing significant headwinds (35% YTD fall, credit downgrade, negative FCF due to massive capex), it boasts an "unprecedented" $638 billion contracted backlog. One analyst (Guggenheim's DiFucci) maintains a $400 price target, seeing a dramatic free cash flow rebound starting in FY2029. This is a high-risk, high-reward play where the market is questioning the timeline of its AI infrastructure bet. If the long-term vision materializes, the upside is substantial.
2.  **Zscaler (ZS):** Down 35% YTD, making it an apparent laggard. However, it has delivered nine consecutive EPS beats, and its CEO explicitly positions it as "ideally positioned as the cybersecurity platform for the AI era." With analysts targeting nearly double its current price, it could be an oversold opportunity with strong underlying fundamentals in a critical AI-driven sector.
3.  **SK Hynix (SKHY):** Extremely volatile but a key player in High-Bandwidth Memory (HBM), essential for AI chips. Its recent 8% rebound and HSBC's "top pick" endorsement, despite sector-wide concerns and macro headwinds, suggest potential for upside. Watch for stabilization and further confirmation of the HBM demand cycle.
4.  **Powell Industries (POWL):** A lesser-known "picks-and-shovels" play for the AI buildout. Despite a recent pullback, its surging new orders, record backlog (including a $400M data center order), and 122% YTD gain highlight its indirect benefit from AI infrastructure spending. It's well-positioned as capital rotates from speculative AI plays to underlying enablers.
5.  **Nvidia (NVDA):** Despite today's negative sentiment and slide, Nvidia remains the leader in the AI chip race. Its significant market capitalization and crucial role in AI development mean any major dip, especially due to profit-taking or market jitters rather than fundamental issues, could present a strategic long-term entry point for those bullish on the continued growth of AI. It's always a bellwether for the AI sector.
