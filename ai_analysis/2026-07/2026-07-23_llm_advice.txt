### 4. Which would you advise me to Keep an Eye On?

Given today's news, here are some key areas and specific stocks to monitor:

*   **The AI Spending Debate (Tech Giants vs. AI Infrastructure):**
    *   **Alphabet (GOOGL), Amazon (AMZN), Meta (META), Microsoft (MSFT), Tesla (TSLA), Oracle (ORCL):** The market is punishing these giants for their escalating AI capital expenditures and potential hit to free cash flow. The critical question is: will these massive investments yield sufficient returns to justify the costs? Their next earnings calls and management commentary on AI ROI will be crucial. This could be a buying opportunity on a dip if the market is overreacting, or a sign of deeper, sustained pressure on margins.
    *   **Intel (INTC) & Dell Technologies (DELL):** These companies are *benefiting* from the same AI spending that's hurting the tech giants. Intel's strong report and upbeat outlook suggest it's well-positioned. Dell's success in AI servers also makes it a strong contender. Watch if they can sustain this growth as AI buildouts continue.
    *   **Former Bitcoin Miners (CIFR, RIOT, HUT, WULF, IREN):** Their pivot to AI compute is currently yielding strong stock performance. Keep an eye on whether they can effectively transition and compete in the AI infrastructure space long-term.
*   **Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) Sector Under Threat:**
    *   **Workday (WKDY), Atlassian (TEAM), HubSpot (HUBS), Salesforce (CRM), Okta (OKTA):** OpenAI's "Presence" product directly competes with established SaaS vendors. This is a significant fundamental shift. Watch how these companies adapt their strategies and whether their stock declines become buying opportunities or a reflection of sustained competitive pressure. ServiceNow (NOW) bucked the trend today, indicating strong execution can still win.
*   **Defense & Aerospace Sector:**
    *   **Lockheed Martin (LMT) & RTX (RTX):** Both delivered stellar earnings and have record backlogs. While they've run hard (up 30%+ YOY), the geopolitical climate suggests continued demand. Watch for sustained government budget commitments and their ability to convert backlog into profitable cash flow. Consider if the "easy money" has been made, but the underlying trend remains strong.
*   **Oil & Gas Majors:**
    *   **Exxon Mobil (XOM) & Chevron (CVX):** Surging oil prices are directly positive for their earnings. However, rising oil prices fuel inflation and increase the likelihood of further interest rate hikes, which could depress the broader market. This is a complex macro play to watch.
*   **IBM (IBM):** The company faces significant challenges with declining legacy businesses. It's a "deep value" consideration for some, but its long-term track record of missing major tech trends makes it a high-risk, high-reward watch. Can CEO Arvind Krishna's AI strategy deliver?
