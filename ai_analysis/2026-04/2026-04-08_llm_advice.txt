### 2. Stocks with Bad Sentiment Today

*   **Exxon Mobil (XOM) & Chevron (CVX):** Both oil giants saw shares drop (XOM down 5%) as crude prices fell following the US-Iran ceasefire agreement, reducing geopolitical risk premiums on oil.
*   **Costco (COST):** Noted as having a "challenging 2025" and one of its "worst relative performances."
*   **MercadoLibre (MELI):** Also described as having a "challenging 2025."
*   **Verizon (VZ):** Received a downgrade from "Buy" to "Hold" by DBS Bank, with upside potential seen as compressed after a 19% year-to-date climb. Concerns include competitive strength from T-Mobile and a substantial $144 billion debt load.
*   **General Dynamics (GD):** Downgraded to "Hold" by Deutsche Bank, receiving a "notable vote of caution."
*   **Energy Sector (General):** The sector overall lost 4.2% as crude prices tumbled due to eased geopolitical tensions.
*   **Private Credit / Software Loans (Sector-Specific):** While not individual public stocks, leading asset managers (Marathon Asset Management, UBS, Morgan Stanley) warned of potential "major losses," rising default rates (up to 15%), and low recovery rates on bad loans in this segment, especially for highly leveraged software makers exposed to AI disruption. This is a significant red flag for the broader financial market.

### 4. Stocks to Keep an Eye On

*   **Meta Platforms (META):** While showing strong gains today due to AI and PayPal partnership, its regulatory headwinds, ongoing Reality Labs losses, and upcoming Q1 2026 earnings (April 29) will be critical for sustaining momentum. Watch for continued AI ad targeting efficiency and clarity on future CapEx guidance.
*   **Verizon (VZ):** The "Hold" downgrade, despite solid fundamentals and an attractive dividend yield (5.7%-5.8%), creates a mixed signal. Monitor if competitive pressures from T-Mobile intensify or if the company makes significant strides in debt reduction. The stock's run-up suggests some may see it as fairly valued, but it could still be a good income play if it dips.
*   **QUALCOMM Incorporated (QCOM):** Being labeled "oversold" can indicate a buying opportunity if its underlying business in data centers and other tech segments continues to perform well. A deep dive into its fundamentals and competitive landscape is warranted.
*   **Oil & Gas Producers (e.g., XOM, CVX):** The significant drop in oil prices due to the ceasefire creates short-term headwind. However, geopolitical situations are fluid. Any renewed tensions or supply disruptions could quickly reverse this trend, making them highly sensitive to global events.
*   **Industrial, Consumer, and Travel Stocks (e.g., SHW, CAT, HD, Southwest, United, Ryanair):** These sectors saw strong rallies today. The question is if this is a short-term "relief rally" or the beginning of sustained growth driven by lower input costs and increased consumer confidence. Monitor for follow-through in the coming days/weeks.
*   **Morgan Stanley (MS) & Crypto-linked equities (COIN, MSTR, GLXY):** Morgan Stanley's entry into the low-fee Bitcoin ETF market is a significant development, indicating mainstream financial adoption. This could bring more institutional money into crypto. Keep an eye on MS for its broader crypto strategy and how this impacts demand for crypto assets and, consequently, crypto-linked companies.
