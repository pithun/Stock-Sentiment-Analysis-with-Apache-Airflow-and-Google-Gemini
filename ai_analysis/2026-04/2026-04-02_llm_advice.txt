### 4. Stocks to Keep an Eye On

These stocks present mixed signals or significant developments that warrant close monitoring:

*   **Apple (AAPL):** While no direct sentiment was given, Apple's 20% stake in Globalstar and control of 85% of its network capacity for Emergency SOS could significantly complicate Amazon's potential acquisition of Globalstar. The outcome will impact Apple's strategic interests.
*   **Johnson & Johnson (JNJ):** Despite strong recent performance and fundamentals, J&J faces looming biosimilar threats (Stelara), new Medicare price caps, and legal overhangs (talc settlement pulled). The interplay between its growth drivers and these structural headwinds will determine future performance.
*   **Pop Mart:** Stock price slid 30% and is down 60% from its August high due to heavy reliance on "The Monsters" IP and falling resale prices. However, Jefferies maintained a "buy" rating with a "solid" growth outlook, and the company is proactively extending IP lifespan with collaborations (Hello Kitty) and a Labubu movie with Sony Pictures. This could be a potential turnaround story.
*   **SanDisk (SNDK):** Recently plummeted 22%, yet Morgan Stanley identified it as one of the "Stocks That Can Make You Richer in 2026." This contradiction suggests short-term pain but potential long-term value, making it crucial to understand the underlying issues.
*   **Tesla (TSLA):** The stock fell on a delivery miss, but the company is deliberately pivoting towards higher-margin robotaxis and humanoid robots, a significant long-term bet. Elon Musk announced FSD version 14.3 rollout, European sales are up, and the energy segment is growing rapidly. Its high P/E ratio means execution on AI and autonomous driving is critical, and insider buying suggests confidence. Highly volatile but with disruptive potential.
*   **Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing (TSM):** Despite strong earnings and bullish analyst sentiment, TSM's stock has pulled back 5% over the past month. This "no longer priced for perfection" scenario ahead of Q1 results makes it an interesting watch for potential entry points if strong results reaffirm the bullish outlook.
*   **Uber (UBER) & Microsoft (MSFT):** These were mentioned as examples of companies where regulatory clouds (UBER) or structural spending concerns (MSFT) are masking strong fundamentals or overriding business expansion. Worth watching how these broader trends evolve.
