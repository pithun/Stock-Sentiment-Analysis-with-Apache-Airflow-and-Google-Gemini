**4. Stocks I Would Advise You to Keep an Eye On:**

These are often high-volatility, sentiment-driven plays worth watching for momentum or potential entry/exit points, but require careful due diligence beyond just Cramer's mention.
*   **Strategy (MSTR) / Stretch (STRC):** Strategy is proposing to shift its perpetual preferred equity (STRC) dividend payment from monthly to semi-monthly to reduce price volatility and enable more consistent capital raising for Bitcoin purchases. While STRC recently fell below its par value, the company's proactive measures to stabilize its stock and buying power for Bitcoin make this an interesting, albeit complex, situation to monitor.
*   **Avis Budget (CAR):** Despite the long-term fundamental concerns, the stock is experiencing an explosive short squeeze (up almost 340% last month). Management is using this technical anomaly for an At-the-Market (ATM) offering. This creates a highly volatile trading environment. Watch for regulatory filings regarding Pentwater's stake and the unwinding of the short squeeze, as this could lead to sharp reversals. **Caution is advised for long-term investors.**
*   **Stocks with Mutual Fund Exits (e.g., ITC, Godrej Properties, Angel One, Wipro, ONGC, Tata Steel, Mankind Pharma, Elecon Engg, Eicher Motors):** Several mutual funds made complete exits from various stocks in March. While fund rebalancing can be for many reasons, a complete institutional exit can sometimes signal a cautious outlook. Investors holding these names might want to investigate the specific reasons behind these fund decisions.
