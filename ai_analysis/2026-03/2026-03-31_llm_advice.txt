#### **4. Stocks to Keep an Eye On**

*   **Amazon (AMZN):** Despite recent declines, the renewed bullish sentiment from Wall Street and the reacceleration of AWS growth suggest a potential turnaround. This could be an entry point if the positive trend continues.
*   **CoreWeave (CRWV):** The bullish analyst call and strong demand outlook, particularly in the AI space, make it a high-growth prospect worth monitoring closely for further developments and market adoption.
*   **UAE Markets (Dubai, Abu Dhabi):** Although significantly impacted, analysts view the current downturn as a "temporary shock." Given their strong economic fundamentals and diversification efforts, these markets could see a "serious rebound" if geopolitical tensions ease.
*   **LG Electronics & Eicher Motors:** Positive analyst recommendations (buy, upgrade) are strong signals. Monitor their performance and any further analyst coverage for potential gains.
*   **Defense Stocks (RTX, Lockheed Martin, Northrup Grumman, Palantir):** These companies saw initial boosts from the conflict but are now subject to ethical and legal scrutiny regarding potential insider trading. Their performance will remain highly reactive to geopolitical developments and any regulatory actions.
*   **Oil Prices (Brent Crude, West Texas Intermediate):** Volatility in oil prices directly impacts inflation and global economic stability. Continuous monitoring is crucial, as any resolution or escalation in the Middle East will profoundly affect them.
*   **Indian Stock Markets (Sensex, Nifty 50):** While currently experiencing FPI outflows, any sign of de-escalation in West Asia or a decline in crude prices could lead to FPIs returning and a market rebound. Domestic institutional investor support provides a partial cushion.
*   **Prediction Market Platforms (Kalshi, Polymarket):** Keep an eye on these not necessarily for direct investment, but because regulatory actions against alleged insider trading could set precedents impacting broader financial market integrity and public trust.
