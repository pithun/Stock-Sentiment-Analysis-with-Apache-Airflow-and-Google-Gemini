*   **SpaceX:** Despite a massive post-IPO rally (up 34-50%), sentiment is overwhelmingly negative regarding its *fundamental valuation*. Michael Burry is avoiding it, calling its $2.8 trillion market cap (130x sales) absurd and "meme-stock territory." Analysts like Morningstar, Truist, and PitchBook warn of extreme overvaluation (Morningstar pegs fair value 70% lower), reliance on untested technologies, significant future spending, and potential volatility. It also reported a $4.3 billion loss in its latest quarter, delaying S&P 500 inclusion.

This strategic move points to underlying investor concerns about "stagnant stock performance" and the company's competitive edge.
*   **MicroStrategy (MSTR - via STRC):** Weakness in its preferred perpetual equity (STRC) reflects low confidence in the company's financial leverage. There are concerns about potential dilution of MSTR holders or reduction of cash reserves to meet dividend payments.

### **4. Stocks to Keep an Eye On**

*   **SpaceX:** This is the *most critical stock to watch* for market observers. While its valuation is fundamentally questionable to many analysts, investor enthusiasm and its entry into the AI sector (via xAI and Cursor acquisitions) make it a highly speculative, high-risk, high-reward play. Its future performance could significantly impact the broader market and the AI boom. Watch for any signs of slowing price momentum or concrete operational news that could justify (or further contradict) its current valuation.
*   **Johnson & Johnson (JNJ):** The growth trajectory of CARVYKTI is a subtle but vital indicator. If the deceleration continues, it could signal headwinds for J&J's ambitious growth targets, potentially leading to a re-evaluation of its premium stock price by the market. Keep an eye on the year-over-year growth percentage of this drug in upcoming earnings reports.
*   **Allbirds (BIRD) / Smartbird:** This stock represents a fascinating and extreme turnaround story. Its complete pivot from footwear to AI infrastructure makes it incredibly volatile. The recent surge and past crash cycles indicate high speculative interest. This is a high-risk, potentially high-reward play for investors willing to closely monitor its execution of the new AI strategy.
*   **Alibaba (BABA) & Zoetis (ZTS):** Michael Burry's decision to increase positions in these companies is a notable signal. Given his "Big Short" fame, his moves often attract attention from value investors. It would be prudent to research the underlying fundamentals and catalysts for these stocks that may have attracted Burry.
